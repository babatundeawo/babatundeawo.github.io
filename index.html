About

Scientist, educator, consultant, and builder.

My expertise includes computational climate models, atmospheric radiative transfer, remote sensing, renewable energy assessment, and visual STEM pedagogy.

Research & publications

Evidence-led work for energy, climate, and education.

My research combines satellite datasets, numerical modeling, and statistical validation to support planning in data-scarce regions.

M.Sc. Thesis • Published 2020

Parametric Estimation of Direct Irradiance under All-Sky Conditions

Developed an all-sky solar radiation estimation method using MODIS satellite datasets, the Yang Model, and cloud transmittance algorithms implemented with Python, MATLAB, and Ferret NOAA.

  • Outperformed NASA SSE products in the study context.
  • Achieved 19.7% annual RMSD compared with ~31.5% for NASA SSE.
  • Validated for renewable energy planning in data-scarce regions.
Solar physics

Solar Structure and Variability

Modeled magnetohydrodynamic and solar hydrodynamic processes with Fortran to explore solar variability and Earth’s magnetospheric interactions.

Renewable energy

Wind Potential Assessment

Applied Weibull and Maximum Entropy distribution functions to evaluate wind characteristics, achieving strong statistical fits with R² values near 0.90–0.99.
